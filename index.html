Watch your cameras on another device

Vigilo can stream the cameras from this computer directly to a phone or laptop — peer to peer, end to end, with nothing going through any server. You connect the two devices once with a pairing code.

How pairing works: the device with the cameras is the Host. The device you want to watch from is the Viewer. The Host makes an invite code, the Viewer replies with a code, you paste it back — done. For watching across the internet, do this while both devices are on Tailscale (setup steps further down).

📡 Host — share this computer's cameras

Do this on the computer that has the webcams. Enable your cameras on the Dashboard first.

📱 Viewer — watch from this device

Do this on the phone or laptop you want to watch from.

Reaching the devices over the internet (Tailscale)

Vigilo has no cloud — by design. To pair when you are away from home, both devices join your own private, encrypted network with Tailscale. It is free for personal use and takes about 5 minutes.

How it works: Tailscale connects your phone and the computer running Vigilo over an encrypted tunnel, as if they were on the same Wi-Fi — even across the internet. Your video never touches anyone else's servers.

Step 1 — Install Tailscale on the host computer

This is the computer running python -m http.server with your cameras plugged in.

  1. Go to tailscale.com/download and install it.
  2. Sign in (Google, Microsoft, GitHub, or email — this only authenticates your devices).
  3. After it connects, note the device's Tailscale IP — it looks like 100.x.x.x. You can see it in the Tailscale app or by running tailscale ip -4.

Step 2 — Install Tailscale on your phone / laptop

  1. Install the Tailscale app on the device you want to watch from (iOS · Android).
  2. Sign in with the same account you used in Step 1.

Step 3 — Start Vigilo with HTTPS

On the host computer, run the launcher (it serves over HTTPS so cameras work on remote devices):

python serve.py

It prints the address to open. Use python3 serve.py if python isn't found.

Step 4 — Open it from your phone

In your phone's browser, with Tailscale switched on, go to:

https://100.x.x.x:8443

Replace 100.x.x.x with the host's Tailscale IP from Step 1. The first visit shows a one-time "not secure" warning (self-signed certificate) — tap Advanced → Proceed. Bookmark it. That's it — encrypted access to your cameras from anywhere.

Two ways to watch remotely: with peer-to-peer pairing (above), the Host runs on its own localhost so its cameras work normally, and the Viewer only receives video — so plain http://100.x.x.x:8000 is enough on the Viewer. If instead you want each device to use its own camera over the network, run python serve.py on the host for automatic HTTPS (browsers only expose cameras on https:// or localhost). Prefer a certificate with no browser warning at all? Use Tailscale's built-in HTTPS: 
tailscale cert        # one-time, enables HTTPS for your tailnet
tailscale serve https / http://localhost:8000
Then open https://your-machine.your-tailnet.ts.net. See Tailscale HTTPS docs.

Same Wi-Fi only? Even simpler

If the viewing device is on the same Wi-Fi as the host, you don't need Tailscale at all. Just run python serve.py on the host and open the "On your phone" address it prints (e.g. https://192.168.1.50:8443) on your phone.

About Vigilo

Vigilo turns the webcams you already own into a private security system. It runs entirely in your browser. There is no backend, no sign-up, and nothing leaves your device.

Quick tips

  • Enable Cameras grants access and shows every detected webcam.
  • Each tile has its own motion sensitivity slider and on/off toggle.
  • Double-click (or use ⛶) on a tile to go fullscreen.
  • Rename a camera by clicking its name.
  • Record saves video to your Downloads folder. Recordings auto-split every 2 hours.
  • Motion-only recording records solely while motion is detected.

What Vigilo does not do

  • No cloud storage or streaming through anyone's servers.
  • No accounts, no email, no telemetry, no analytics.
  • No external requests — it works fully offline.
  • Your password and settings live in this browser's localStorage only.
Vigilo is free and open source under the MIT license. Tinker with it, audit it, make it yours.
