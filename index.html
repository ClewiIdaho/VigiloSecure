Watch and control your cameras from your phone

One computer — the one with the webcams — becomes the Home Hub. Any other device, like your phone or a laptop, connects to it and gives you the full dashboard: live video, motion alerts, recording, and every setting. There are no codes to copy. You connect once and it works.

Checking remote access…

The short version:
  1. On the computer with the cameras: run python serve.py, open the address it prints, click "Enable Cameras," then turn on the "Home Hub" switch in the toolbar.
  2. On your phone: open the same address (use Tailscale if you are away from home). Vigilo finds the hub and connects on its own, and you land right on the live cameras.
One thing to know: you must start the computer with python serve.py. The plain python -m http.server command does not include the helper that makes the phone connection work.

Connecting when you are away from home (Tailscale)

If your phone and computer are on the same Wi-Fi, you do not need anything else. To connect from somewhere else, both devices join your own private network using Tailscale. It is free for personal use and takes about five minutes to set up.

What Tailscale does: it links your phone and your home computer over an encrypted connection, as if they were on the same Wi-Fi, even across the internet. Your video still never touches anyone else's servers.

Step 1 — Install Tailscale on the home computer

This is the computer running python serve.py with your cameras plugged in.

  1. Go to tailscale.com/download and install it.
  2. Sign in. You can use Google, Microsoft, GitHub, or email; this only confirms which devices are yours.
  3. Once it connects, find this computer's Tailscale address. It looks like 100.x.x.x. You can see it in the Tailscale app, or by running tailscale ip -4.

Step 2 — Install Tailscale on your phone or laptop

  1. Install the Tailscale app on the device you want to watch from (iPhone or Android).
  2. Sign in with the same account you used in Step 1.

Step 3 — Start Vigilo on the home computer

Run the launcher. It serves Vigilo over HTTPS, which is what lets the camera work on other devices:

python serve.py

It prints the address to open. Use python3 serve.py if python does not work.

Step 4 — Open it on your phone

In your phone's browser, with Tailscale turned on, go to:

https://100.x.x.x:8443

Replace 100.x.x.x with the home computer's Tailscale address from Step 1. The first visit shows a one-time "not secure" warning, because the certificate is one Vigilo made itself. It is safe here because you are only connecting to your own computer. Tap "Advanced," then "Proceed." Bookmark the page so you do not have to type it again.

About that warning: serve.py uses a certificate it made itself, so each device shows the "not secure" prompt once. Tap "Advanced," then "Proceed," and you are set. If you would rather not see the warning at all, Tailscale can give you a trusted certificate for free: 
tailscale cert
tailscale serve https / http://localhost:8443
Then open https://your-machine.your-tailnet.ts.net. Details are in the Tailscale HTTPS guide.

On the same Wi-Fi? It is even simpler

If the phone is on the same Wi-Fi as the computer, you do not need Tailscale at all. Just run python serve.py on the computer and open the "On your phone" address it prints (for example https://192.168.1.50:8443) on your phone.

About Vigilo

Vigilo turns the webcams you already own into a home security system. It runs entirely in your browser. There is no backend, no sign-up, and nothing leaves your device.

How to use it

  • Enable Cameras asks for camera permission and shows every webcam it finds.
  • Each camera has its own motion sensitivity slider and an on/off button.
  • To make a camera fullscreen, double-click it or use the ⛶ button.
  • To rename a camera, click its name and type a new one.
  • Record saves video to your Downloads folder. Long recordings are split into a new file every 2 hours.
  • Motion-only recording records only while something is moving.
  • Home Hub (needs python serve.py) shares this computer's cameras so your phone can watch and control them from anywhere. See the Remote Access tab.

What Vigilo does not do

  • No cloud. Your footage, accounts, and settings never go to anyone else's servers.
  • No accounts, no email, no tracking, no analytics.
  • No third-party requests. Remote access uses a small helper that runs only on your own computer, and the video goes straight between your devices.
  • Your password and settings stay in this browser only.
